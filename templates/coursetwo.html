Fundamentals of Agroecology


There are 13 principles of Agroecology:

  • Biodiversity: Maintain and increase the variety of species, genetic resources, and functional diversity.
  • Soil Health: Improve soil health and functioning to support plant growth.
  • Input Reduction: Reduce or eliminate reliance on purchased inputs.
  • Economic Diversification: Increase financial independence and value addition opportunities for small-scale farmers.
  • Recycling: Recycle nutrients through composting and other natural methods.
  • Participation: Encourage others to get involved and provide input on how agricultural and food systems are managed.
  • Synergy: Enhance positive ecological interactions between elements.
  • Animal Health: Ensure animal health and welfare.
  • Co-Creation of Knowledge: Create knowledge together.
  • Social Values and Diets: Consider social values and diets.
  • Fairness: Promote fairness.
  • Connectivity: Promote connectivity.
  • Land and Natural Resource Governance: Manage land and natural resources responsibly.
